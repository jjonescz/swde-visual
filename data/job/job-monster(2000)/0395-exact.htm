About the Job

We have a passion for life at INTEGRITYOne Partners (IOP).  All of life.  Our team enjoys a demanding, exhilarating professional life balanced by a family-friendly, flexible work schedule with minimal travel.  We’ve been recognized for designing a consulting culture that not only supports your career ambitions, but your whole life.  At IOP, we actively encourage you to pursue your passions outside of work.  

At IOP we don’t chase every bid, and we don’t do staff augmentation - we focus on projects that make a difference.  We look for challenging projects,  to a large extent are ahead of the technology curve, that enable our consultants to fully engage.  Our work is not purely theoretical - working here, you’ll develop prototype applications in support of architectural proof, using a variety of technologies. You will not be creating “shelf-ware” – you’ll be turning your vision into practical reality, creating real solutions for real business problems.  You will interpret business problems and determine the correct technology solutions, understanding the implications of putting technologies together but never forgetting the secondary nature of technology – knowing that the real problems we solve are business problems.  We’re counting on your good instincts with technology, your ability to analyze a variety of scenarios, figure out what is not being done, and see where the gaps are in any solution.  You’ll need the technical breadth to know where the pitfalls are in a solution, and the depth to be confident that you are not underestimating the difficulties of your work. 

In this role you will need to be fearless in front of a white board, able to explain technical concepts in a non-technical way and defend your proposed solutions without becoming defensive.

Internally, within IOP, we enjoy spirited debate.  We encourage diversity of thought and welcome any degree of your engagement in shaping our firm’s culture and growth trajectory.  Working here, your voice will be heard, your thoughts will be welcomed, and your contributions will matter.


Responsibilities of an Architect / Engineer:

 

In this role you will:

  • Analyze high-level requirements and translate them to overall system-level design - to be used for architectural prescription, cost / benefit analysis, and concept development.
  • Identify, define, and communicate design tradeoffs using a structured, detail-oriented approach.
  • Look beyond individual projects to coordinate design approaches, standards, and architectural vision across the enterprise
  • Analyze existing systems environment to determine applicability, benefits, and drawbacks in support of new requirements.
  • Develop prototype applications in support of architectural proof using a variety of technologies as dictated by architectural prescription.
  • Support project management activities including analysis, planning, project control, reporting, and evaluations.
  • Support internal initiatives that  sustain the growth of the firm.
  • Support and build deep client relationships.

Required Experience:

  • 12+ years of Architectural experience. 
  • Experience as lead architect or technical lead on several enterprise-level development efforts.
  • Experience with technologies at multiple levels of the architecture, including some or all of the following: user interface, applications, database, security and/or network.
  • Experience with some or all of the following technologies: J2EE, .Net, RDBMS, Business Intelligence, Web Services, SOA, and/or Web 2.0.
  • Experience in solving complex business issues.
  • Experience working directly with clients.
  • Strong oral and written communication skills.

 

Desired Experience:  

  • Experience supporting multiple Federal agencies, particularly ICE division of DHS and the FDA division of HHS.
  • Big Four or similar Consulting Background (Government / Commercial).
  • Strong client management / client relationship skills
  • Understanding of project management principles
  • Ability to work in a team environment
  • Experience with agile development methodologies.  
  • Experience supporting Business Development and proposals.
  • Experience leading small teams or projects.
  • Active security clearance.

 

Other Requirements:

  • The ability to obtain and maintain a Secret level clearance is required to be considered for this position. (Requires US Citizenship and you will be subject to a government security investigation.)
  • Bachelor’s degree.
  • Advanced facilitation and mediation skills.
  • Strong analytical and problem solving skills.
  • Ability to communicate clearly and put together materials for technical and non-technical audiences that include project members and executives.

Attributes of an Architect / Engineer:

 

·         You are service driven and highly organized.  You have the ability to prioritize and manage multiple projects simultaneously, setting expectations carefully, honoring your deadlines and keeping your promises.  You skillfully manage details.

·         You consistently demonstrate humble confidence, professionalism, tact, diplomacy and good judgment. 

·         You are naturally curious, comfortable exploring new solutions, and you employ a sound decision making process when evaluating new technologies.

·         You are analytical - able to take functions and requirements, decompose them into solutions and logically articulate how you got there. 

·         Your flexibility allows you to adjust to changing priorities, operate effectively in a fluid environment and anticipate and meet deadlines.  

 

What’s Attractive about Integrity One Partners:

 

Our employees report that they “get their life back” when they join IOP:

 

·         We know the right way to run a project profitably - we don’t cut corners and under-staff projects to maintain profitability.

·         We don’t have proposal “death marches” at IOP – we don’t blindly pursue every possible bid to drive revenue growth.  We create work/life balance for our employees by narrowing our focus to delivering high-end, high-impact work, not bidding on proposals we cannot win.  We deliberately pursue only those projects we can deliver profitably and deliver well. You won’t work on 12 proposals a year; you’ll work on 2 or 3. 

·         When we do choose to pursue a bid, you won’t have to do everything yourself.  At IOP, business development is not something you’ll do “on top of everything else” – it’s run like a project and it’s a team effort.  You’ll have enthusiastic assistance from our business development teams and often, depending on your workload, won’t even need to lead the effort, but can take the SME role.

·         At IOP, you will have the benefit of being surrounded by smart and technically strong colleagues who enjoy stimulating discussions, and sometimes spirited debates, about current and emerging technology.

 

We look out for your career, and your reputation:

 

·         IOP has a very clear, formal career path program.  We take your career as seriously as you do, and we actively look for projects where our team members can develop new skills to further their careers.   

·         Our sophisticated performance management process takes your personal goals into account, naturally aligning them with company goals – giving you a balanced, satisfying professional life. 

·         Deep relationships with our customers are important to us. We don’t go in with a pre-set agenda. 

·         We invest time orienting new employees to help them come up to speed and “learn the lay of the land” on projects.  You won’t be embarrassed in front of a client by walking in unprepared.

Our environment is stimulating, challenging, fun and collegial.  Good ideas are readily accepted here. As a result, our office is filled with client service awards.

 

Our commitment to service is not limited to our clients; we also believe in making a positive impact through our community service.
