												<p><strong>Duties &amp; Responsibilities:&nbsp; </strong>Harvard University's Office of the University CIO (UCIO) is seeking an Oracle Applications Developer to be part of the Finance and Procurement Systems practice.&nbsp; This practice is part of the Office of Administrative Systems (OAS) and is responsible for maintaining Harvard University's enterprise-wide applications that support the Finance and Procurement business community campus-wide.<a name="OLE_LINK2"></a><a name="OLE_LINK1"></a></p> <p>We seek to hire a team-oriented developer with recent implementation and/or support experience in a Release 12 E-Business Suite environment.&nbsp; The successful candidate must be a developer with deep experience with Oracle's Application Framework (OAF), including both extending and personalizing one or more of Oracle's self-service modules.&nbsp; In addition, proven working knowledge of BI Publisher is another skill we are seeking in this candidate.</p> <p>A strong working knowledge of Oracle's E-Business Suite applications is required for this position.&nbsp; Our current portfolio of modules includes:&nbsp; AP, GL, AR, iProcurement, Purchasing and Cash Management.&nbsp; We also develop, support and maintain a number of major custom-developed applications built to integrate with the Oracle Application infrastructure (P-Card, Web Voucher, Chart Request System, Capital Project Approval System).&nbsp; We also provide technical support for a number of smaller 3<sup>rd</sup> party applications (Paybase/Bottomline, Glacier, SciQuest integration, Credit Card integrations, InputAcel (Imaging).)</p> <p>The key responsibilities for this position include the following:</p> <ul> <li>Working with other developers on the team to develop, support and maintain customizations and extensions to Harvard's E-Business Suite Applications </li> <li>Working with business analysts to design and develop technical solutions to new business problems </li> <li>Providing level of effort estimates for technical activities to assist with planning efforts </li> <li>Writing technical specifications in response to business requirements </li> <li>Developing and testing technical solutions </li> <li>Prototyping concepts to assist with feasibility analysis </li> <li>Troubles-shooting production issues </li> <li>Participating in day/night on-call support rotation</li> </ul> <p>Other activities include but are not limited to the following:</p> <ul> <li>Researching bugs and issues with Oracle Support Analysts </li> <li>Browsing online forums, etc. to find additional information related to issues or products </li> <li>Sharing ideas and experiences with colleagues at other Higher Education institutions </li> <li>Implementing new modules in the future to address the University's business needs. (iExpense, iSupplier, Fixed Assets, Approvals Management, User Management to name a few modules that we are considering.)</li> </ul> 
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>Basic Qualifications:&nbsp; </strong>To be considered for this role, candidates must have the following experience and skills:</p> <p>A bachelor's degree is required, preferable in computer science.</p> <p>Applicants must have strong demonstrated programming experience (at least 5 years) in support of Oracle's E-Business Suite.&nbsp; To be considered you must be able to demonstrate strong proficiency in various aspects of Oracle Applications technology and architecture, with a strong emphasis on:</p> <p>OAF (Priority)</p> <p>BI Publisher (Priority)</p> <p>J-Developer (Priority)</p> <p>XML</p> <p>PL/SQL</p> <p>Java/J2EE</p> <p>AOL</p> <p>Unix Shell Scripting</p> <p>Full Software Development Lifecycle</p> <p>You will be responsible for streamlining the system's processes, and enhancing its performance.&nbsp; You will be required to adhere to production support processes and implementation change management procedures.</p> <p><strong>Additional Qualifications:&nbsp; </strong>In addition to the required skills, the ideal candidate will have these highly desirable experiences and/or skills:</p> <ul> <li>Support and maintenance experience with two or more of the E-Biz modules implemented at Harvard (AR, GL, AP, iProcurement, Purchasing, Cash Management) </li> <li>On-the-Job experience with Version 12 of E-Business Suite. </li> <li>HTML </li> <li>Workflow </li> <li>BPEL </li> <li>Open Interfaces and Oracle API's </li> <li>Oracle Developer tools 10g (nice to have) </li> <li>PVCS or another Source Code Control utility </li> <li>Eclipse (nice to have) </li> <li>Oracle Applications Server</li> </ul> <p>Web Logic / Apache</p> <p><strong>Additional Information:&nbsp; </strong>Please note:&nbsp; Harvard University requires pre-employment reference and background screening.&nbsp; The Office of the University CIO is unable to provide work authorization and/or visa sponsorship. <strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong>This position has a 180-day probationary period.</p> <p>Please apply at <a href="http://www.employment.harvard.edu">www.employment.harvard.edu</a> req# 22323</p>
