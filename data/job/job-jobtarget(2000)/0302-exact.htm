												<b>About the Department</b>:<br /> The HANC Legacy Project works to increase the participation of African Americans and Hispanics in HIV prevention and treatment clinical research by building on the current efforts and successes of the ongoing HIV Vaccine Trials Network Legacy Project. We are committed to extensive work on partnership and relationship development to build bridges of trust, increasing engagement and collaborative efforts between the populations most impacted and researchers and their institutions while addressing historic inequities to overcome the demographics and socio-economic disparities that threaten all DAIDS-funded research and ultimately the communities and populations they serve.<br /><br /><b>About Us</b>:<br /> Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center, home of three Nobel laureates, is an independent, nonprofit research institution dedicated to the development and advancement of biomedical research to eliminate cancer and other potentially fatal diseases. Recognized internationally for its pioneering work in bone-marrow transplantation, the Centers four scientific divisions collaborate to form a unique environment for conducting basic and applied science. The Hutchinson Center, in collaboration with its clinical and research partners, the University of Washington and Childrens Hospital and Regional Medical Center, is the only National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center in the Pacific Northwest. Join us and make a difference.<br /><br /><b>Equal Employment Opportunity</b>:<br /> The Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center and The Seattle Cancer Care Alliance are equal opportunity employers, committed to workforce diversity.<br /><br /><b>Qualifications</b>:<br /> Doctoral-level education in behavioral or social sciences, medicine, or related field and a minimum of 3 years experience in conducting research on, or developing and implementing clinical research in HIV/AIDS, STDS or a related field of study.&nbsp; Additional experience in training and the development of communication programs and materials, and/or community advocacy is desirable.&nbsp; Experience must reflect mature level of knowledge, skills and materials development. <br /><br />Must demonstrate past organizational leadership, program management and fluency for complex organization with a research agenda.&nbsp; Must have participated in the design and conduct of clinical research.<br /><br />The ideal candidate should be highly organized; should have exceptional written and verbal communication skills; should have experience in managing multidisciplinary teams; should be able to handle challenging workloads and multiple concurrent projects; and should be proficient with standard business productivity software, including word processing, spreadsheet, presentation graphics, and database applicants.&nbsp; Experience with database management software packages would be an asset.<br /><br />Ability to relate to and coordinate multidisciplinary and culturally diverse research, technical and management staff.<br /><br />Sensitivity to cultural, ethnic and gender differences.<br /><br /><b>Major Duties</b>:<br /> 1.&nbsp; Provide leadership to HANC Legacy Project in development of scientific methodology for operations and program planning related to serving special populations. <br /><br />2.&nbsp; Work with network investigators to conduct research that establishes best practices in the development of interventions and program policy.<br /> <br />3.&nbsp; Ensure all parties maximize research methodology based on biobehavioral and sociocultural dynamics in diverse populations towards the goal of ensuring participation of traditionally underrepresented populations.<br /><br />4.&nbsp; Work with HANC, network leadership and the HANC Legacy Project Manager collaboratively in the planning, development and execution of all aspects of network programs with a focus on the respective network research agendas to enhance the participation of traditionally underrepresented populations in HIV clinical therapeutics and prevention research.<br /><br />5.&nbsp; Work collaboratively with the HVTNs Legacy Project.<br /><br />6.&nbsp; Serve as an advisor on controversial, complex, or unprecedented matters in relation to research that includes special populations. Participates in the identification of resources that enhance the networks ability to conduct research.<br /><br />7.&nbsp; Attend meetings, workshops, conferences and symposia to represent HANC and help others stay abreast of developments relevant to serving target populations and foster the aims of the HANC Legacy Project. <br /><br />8.&nbsp; Demonstrate working knowledge of a broad range of scientific, managerial and supervisory activities directed at HIV clinical prevention and therapeutic research.&nbsp; <br /><br />9.&nbsp; Identify opportunities for HANC and the networks to increase mobilization of different audiences to increase understanding of complex research agendas.<br /><br />10.&nbsp; Travel required to accomplish goals of this position<br /><br />11.&nbsp; Perform other responsibilities as assigned.<br /><br /><b>Responsibilities</b>:<br /> Provides scientific leadership to Office of HIV/AIDS Network Coordinations (HANC) Legacy Project and scientific programs to ensure activities are designed to maximize participation of traditionally underserved populations. Primary focus of activity is 5 of the DAIDS-funded HIV clinical trials networks (ACTG, HPTN, IMPAACT, INSIGHT and MTN) and their U.S. research sites regarding: protocol operations, communications, community mobilization and policy development to ensure scientifically sound and culturally appropriate activities.&nbsp; Conducts research and develops a research agenda to identify covariates that account for racial disparities and interventions needed to reduce disparities. Manages research agenda in cooperation with the HANC Director and the HANC Legacy Project Manager. The HANC Legacy Project works in collaboration and coordination with the HVTNs Legacy Project.<br /><br />The HANC Legacy Project Scientific Director (SD) provides leadership, expert advice and guidance to HANC, the DAIDS-funded HIV clinical trials networks and clinical trial unit investigators in developing scientific research that improves the ability to serve special populations and reduce racial or ethnic disparities.&nbsp; The primary focus is to develop and conduct a behavioral and social science research agenda that produces a theoretically and empirically driven model of research participation that will guide the recruitment and retention efforts of underrepresented populations.&nbsp; The SD leads and coordinates potential multisite grant proposals for the DAIDS HIV clinical trials networks and US sites and applies for grants that further the overall research agenda. This will also lead to identifying, addressing and resolving operational issues to improve participation and education of special target groups. The SD conducts these activities in collaboration with the HANC Legacy Project Work Group. The SD will also work collaboratively on joint projects with the HVTNs Legacy Project and the HVTNs Legacy Social Scientist.<br /><br />The SD works with minimal supervision and is expected to use initiative and sound judgment in carrying-out responsibilities within the framework of HANC policies and procedures. The SD represents HANC at meetings, workshops, conferences and symposia and fosters internal and external communications and relationships to increase service to target populations and foster the aims of the HANC Legacy Project.<br /><br /><b>Other Information</b>:<br /> Job #AD-22943<br /><br /><b>Pay,Benefits, &amp; Work Schedule</b>:<br /> Salary DOE+ excellent benefits; Part-Time Position; 50&#037; FTE<br /><br /><b>How To Apply</b>:<br /> To apply for this position please click on the &quot;Apply Now&quot; button below.&nbsp; If you have not previously registered with us you will be prompted to do so, otherwise please follow the online directions to complete your application.Job ID: 22943
