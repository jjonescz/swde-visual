									<div id="jt_job_description">
										
											
												<p><strong>Requisition Number: </strong>11481BR<br /><br /><strong>Department: </strong>YMG System Support<br /><br /><strong>University Generic Title: </strong>Business Systems Analyst 2<br /><br /><strong>Type of Employment: </strong>Full Time<br /><br /><strong>Duration Type: </strong>Regular<br /><br /><strong>Work Week: </strong>Standard - 37.5 hrs (M-F, 8:30-5:00)<br /><br /><strong>Work Location: </strong>Medical School Campus<br /><br /><strong>Worksite Address: </strong>300 George Street<br /><br /><strong>Position General Purpose: </strong><br />Under the general direction of the Yale Medical Group (YMG) Manager of GE Applications, this position is responsible for development, support and training for new and existing GE Centricity Business applications and other supporting information systems. Responsibilities include; developing programs, systems and applications and the related procedures and training to process data for the YMG; formulate and define systems scope and objectives through interviews with users to determine data needs; enhance applications to meet these needs; maintain existing applications and training programs; coordinate and perform specification and flow chart development; analyze system-related problems and help desk calls, and resolve or propose solutions; participate in the testing of new systems; development of system documentation and business procedures; and training of staff in utilization of systems.<br /><br /><strong>Essential Duties: </strong><br />1. Train users on all GE Centricity Business applications and non-GE applications as requested and needed. (i.e. Microsoft Word, Excel, reporting system, customer service).<br />2. Develop; prepare and deliver documentation for all training curriculum. <br />3. Analyze user needs and create proposals for new training programs or systems. <br />4. Assist in planning, testing, and implementation of GE upgrades or new GE functionality. This includes the planning of customizations of software to meet Yale Medical Groups needs, bug-testing, producing documentation for training and training all end users on the final product.<br />5. Provide informational input into the development of solutions to problems referred to by other YMG staff. Serve as a liaison with clinical department end users for problem solving, education or enhancements.<br />6. Keep current in emerging training technologies, advancements and strategies through involvement in professional organizations, seminar attendance, user group meetings and research.<br />7. Provide initiative and assistance resolving issues and questions in the functionality of IDX and supporting systems.<br />8. Develop training scenarios in the training database as necessary for classes and testing purposes. Maintain the integrity of the training database data.<br />9. Coordinate GE security access for all users.<br />10. Design and distribute monthly newsletters to YMG staff. <br />11. Provide follow-up with trained users through refresher training classes, communication with managers and conducting monthly rounds to all sites.<br />12. Test, troubleshoot and perform related system refinements and improvements.<br />13. Identify process inefficiencies; recommend and implement system enhancements and/or processing changes to optimize productivity.<br />14. Develop solutions to technical problems referred to by other YMG staff. Serve as a liaison with clinical department end users for problem solving, education or enhancements.<br />15. Provide initiative for resolving issues and questions in the functionality of GE and supporting systems.<br />16. Provide leadership in the development and implementation of recommendations to achieve desired results.<br />17. May serve as the primary support person for a particular business system.</p>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>Education and Experience: </strong><br />1. Bachelor's Degree and four years of experience in the analysis of business needs and development of related business systems.<br /><br /><strong>Additional Education and Experience: </strong><br />1. Experience in training development and instruction or billing and/or operations.<br />2. Knowledge and understanding of technology including, but not limited to, database design and logic.<br />3. Preferred: Knowledge of physician-based group practices and information systems; insurance and/or Healthcare Operations background. (HMO, PPO, IPA, etc.); working knowledge of regulatory bodies and compliance issues (i.e. HIPAA, NCQA); experience with ANSI standard for EDI transaction sets.<br /><br /><strong>Skills &amp; Abilities: </strong><br />1. Superior interpersonal, oral and written communication skills and the ability to communicate with various levels of employees (administrative to staff) within the organization.<br />2. Thorough knowledge of information systems and the applicable business systems applications and reporting tools.<br />3. Working knowledge of Microsoft Excel, Word and Access. <br />4. Ability to analyze and evaluate policies, methods and procedures.<br />5. Preferred: "Charismatic" approach to teaching which solicits input from students and validates the information being presented.<br /><br /><strong>Background Check Requirements: </strong>All external candidates for employment will be subject to pre-employment background screening for this position, which may include motor vehicle and credit checks based on the position description and job requirements. All offers are contingent on successful completion of a background check. Please visit www.yale.edu/hronline/careers/screening/faqs.html for additional information on the background check requirements and process.<br /><br /><br />Yale University is an affirmative action/equal opportunity employer. Yale values diversity in its faculty, staff, and students and strongly encourages applications from women and members of underrepresented minority groups.</p>
