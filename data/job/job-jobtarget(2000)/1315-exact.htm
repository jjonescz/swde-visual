												<p>BC Main Campus<br /><br />Boston College is a leading national Jesuit, Catholic university, enrolling 14,700 students, 9,000 full-time undergraduates and 4,900 graduate and professional students. Located six miles from downtown Boston, the University has 760 full-time faculty, 2,500 employees, an operating budget of $808 million, and an endowment of $1.6 billion.<br /><br />Department: 021601 - Facilities Management - Planning Department<br />Position: 00012519 - Clerk of the Works<br />Grade or Band: 3RD<br />This is a two-year, full-time position.<br /><br />Under the direction of the Project Manager, the Clerk of the Works will be responsible for daily on-site observation, reporting and coordination of construction activities for Major Projects including Stokes Hall project (a 183,000 gsf Classroom &amp; Faculty office building).&nbsp; Besides providing&nbsp; on-site monitoring services, the Clerk provides daily inspections, coordination of shutdowns, communications with occupants/abutters, preparation of regular field reports and logs.&nbsp; Additionally, the Clerk will provide regular monitoring of the construction activities to ensure compliance with drawings/specifications as well as code requirements, monitoring of progress according to the construction schedule, and providing overall quality control.&nbsp; <br /><br />The Clerk will assist the Project Manager with the review and processing of submittals, and the processing of additional project documentation such as invoices, meeting minutes, testing reports, commissioning reports, A/E field reports, etc.&nbsp; The Clerk will have a critical role in the process of systems commissioning, owner systems training, monitoring of the final punch list, and the project close-out documentation. The Clerk will assist the Project Manager in the coordination of oversight of Owner FF&amp;E and move-in activities.<br /><br />This position requires on-site construction experience, a working knowledge of design, plans, specifications, and construction practices for large scale new construction.&nbsp; The position requires a working knowledge of federal, state and local building codes, as well as an understanding of ADA and MAAB. The position also requires prior experience with MEP systems in terms of&nbsp; system installation, system start-up, and commissioning. Requires familiarity with OSHA and hazardous materials. Ability to wear/use personal protective equipment as required on construction site.<br /><br />The successful candidate must demonstrate an ability to handle complex problems, be objective, maintain tact and flexibility. The candidate must demonstrate a high degree of organization, follow-through, and complete record keeping. <br /><br />In addition, familiarity with project estimating, scheduling, and cash flow management is essential.&nbsp; Experience with Word, Excel is needed; familiarity with Project Management software is desired.<br /><br />A minimum of 8 years of recent experience in construction is required, with prior experience as Clerk of the Works for new construction of similar type and scale. An understanding of how to build or renovate is essential. Prior experience working in a stipulated sum approach is required. Prior experience in a university setting is preferred.<br /><br />Boston College is an affirmative action, equal opportunity employer.<br /><br /><strong>Regular/Temporary</strong>: Temporary</p>
