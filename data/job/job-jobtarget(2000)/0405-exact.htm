												<center><strong>Graduate Fellowship and Assistantship Coordinator<br />Graduate College<br />University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign</strong></center> <p><br /> <br />The Graduate College has oversight responsibilities for about 9,000 students pursuing degrees in over 300 Master's and doctoral programs in more than a dozen academic colleges, schools, and institutes. The College works closely with individual graduate students, graduate faculty, graduate program administrative staff, academic colleges, campus administration, Office of the Registrar, Student Affairs units, and various college and campus committees to address a wide range of issues basic to graduate education at Illinois. The Graduate College is also committed to creating new knowledge through research on graduate education, synthesizing national best practices, and participating in national and international dialog on innovations in graduate education. <br /><br /><strong>The Fellowship and Assistantship Coordinator in the Graduate College is a regular 12-month, full-time (100percent) academic professional reporting to and working closely with the Executive Assistant Dean to manage, supervise and coordinate the Fellowship Office.</strong> The Coordinator will supervise staff and have oversight for the Fellowship competitions and for the appointment processes for all Fellows. The Coordinator will oversee the daily processing flow for these and associated areas and will coordinate services with departments and administrative offices. The activities associated with this position affect over 1,200 graduate students, and hundreds more, including faculty, all over campus who call or visit the office every day.<br /><br /><strong>Coordinate Fellowship Office</strong>: Develop and maintain a customer service environment; Oversee daily operations; Manage fellowship@grad.illinois.edu mailbox; Review, approve, and designate appropriate coding for all fellowship appointments processed by the Graduate College; Develop procedures and administer policies for Fellows and Assistants; Develop and maintain content for Fellowship pages on Graduate College website; Establish, coordinate, and maintain reporting system related to all services provided; Review traineeship and fellowship grant proposals and communicate with graduate programs regarding appropriate policies; Work with Graduate College and campus units on processes to provide assistants and fellows with appropriate benefits (summer health benefits, dental and vision insurance); and Independently counsel students, assessing their needs and directing them to the appropriate aid resources and troubleshoot billing problems.<br /><br /><strong>Personnel Management</strong>: Participate in the selection process, train and provide direct supervision of Fellowship staff; Supervise daily workflow of staff; Conduct weekly staff meetings to provide information; Assist the Executive Assistant Dean with internal controls for the fellowship area, making sure policies are followed and problems are addressed promptly; Develop a strong support team; Provide on-going feedback to staff and conduct annual performance evaluations.<br /><br /><strong>Coordinate and communicate with graduate programs and campus administrative offices</strong>: Develop and maintain strong communications with graduate departments, campus administrative offices and students via phone, email, and in person regarding Graduate College policies and procedure; Attend college and campus-wide meetings regarding Fellowship and Assistantship policies; Develop, participate in, and facilitate policy and process training for staff in graduate departments through written materials, the Web, and workshops; Provide administrative support to the Executive Assistant Dean and the Director of External Fellowships in preparation for workshop presentations. <br /><br /><strong>Fiscal Management</strong>: Collaborate with the Graduate College Budget Office with the preparation of reports; Provide initial review of reports submitted by returning Dissertation Travel Grant awardees prior to submission to the Budget Office for payment; and Troubleshoot for departments and students needing assistance in fiscal matters concerning fellowships and/or assistantships.</p>

The individual will have proven ability to interpret and execute academic policy; to develop and write procedures that correspond and adhere to policy; and to provide and coordinate services with diverse groups of people in a team-oriented environment. Strong communications and interpersonal skills are required. The individual must demonstrate the ability to take initiative and act independently within policy guidelines.<br /><strong>Preferred</strong>: Preference is given to current University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign candidates.
