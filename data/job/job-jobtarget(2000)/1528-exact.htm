												<p align="center"><strong>FACULTY POSITION, NEUROIMAGING</strong></p> <p>Saint Louis University, a Catholic, Jesuit university dedicated to student learning,<br />research, health care and community service, is seeking a scientist at an open rank level for a<br />non-tenure track, grant-funded faculty position in the Division of Neurosurgery, Department of<br />Surgery at Saint Louis University School of Medicine.</p> <p><br />The successful candidate will be a leader in the Division&rsquo;s neuroimaging research using the<br />installed 248-channel magnetoencephalography (MEG) system and 3 Tesla MRI. The Division<br />has several ongoing projects, including a Department of Defense funded study of traumatic brain<br />injury and the MEG component of the Human Connectome Project. A team of research staff<br />members has been assembled to perform studies assisted by graduate and post-graduate students.<br />The ideal candidate would hold a Ph.D. in any of the following areas: biomedical engineering,<br />physiology, experimental/cognitive psychology, neuroscience, or other applicable disciplines.<br />Previous experience in functional neuroimaging is expected, including familiarity with standard<br />computational software packages. Primary responsibilities would include comparison and<br />integration of multiple neuroimaging modalities; statistical analyses using neuroimaging data; and<br />preparation of presentations and manuscripts to disseminate findings. There also may be a need<br />for supervision of other staff and guidance of students working on research projects. Preferred<br />candidates would have significant neuroscience research experience, but other less experienced<br />applicants (i.e. individuals who have completed a post-doctoral fellowship or equivalent in the<br />neurosciences or related fields) will be considered and are encouraged to apply. Salary will be<br />commensurate with academic credentials and experience. Candidates will be expected to apply<br />for funding from external agencies to continue the work of the Division in the area of<br />neuroimaging using the installed base of equipment.</p> <p><br />Interested candidates should submit a cover letter describing current research interests and goals,<br />application, relevant reprints, and current curriculum vitae to http://jobs.slu.edu. Also, a cover<br />letter and curriculum vitae should be sent to:</p> <p><br />Richard Bucholz, MD, FACS<br />Director and Professor, Division of Neurosurgery<br />Department of Surgery<br />Saint Louis University<br />3635 Vista Ave.<br />St. Louis, MO 63110-0250<br />Phone: 314-577-8795<br />Email: Richard@bucholz.org</p> <p><br />Saint Louis University is an affirmative action, equal opportunity employer, and encourages<br />nominations and applications of women and minorities.</p>
