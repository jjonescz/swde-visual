											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>Requires: <br /> KNOWLEDGE OF:&nbsp;</strong> <br /> Principles and practices of community college administration.&nbsp; Principles and practices of business services management and collective bargaining in the public sector.&nbsp; District and college organization, operations, policies and objectives.&nbsp; State and federal laws, codes and regulations related to business administration of a public education agency, including the California Education Code and the California Government Code.&nbsp; District policies, operational procedures and guidelines applicable to the administration of a comprehensive public sector business services program.&nbsp;&nbsp; Business services information systems functions and operations.&nbsp; Negotiation techniques and public sector collective bargaining processes.&nbsp; Progressive discipline procedures and documentation.&nbsp; Budget preparation and control.&nbsp; Oral and written communication skills.&nbsp; Interpersonal skills including tact, patience and diplomacy.&nbsp; Computer systems and software applications related to business services.<br /> <br /> <strong>ABILITY TO: </strong><br /> Plan, develop, organize, coordinate, administer, monitor, control and evaluate a wide variety of District business services functions and administrative services, operations, programs and activities.&nbsp; Provide leadership in assigned business services areas.&nbsp; Participate in negotiating collective bargaining agreements.&nbsp; Develop and administer department budget.&nbsp; Develop and administer policies and procedures for business services.&nbsp; Work cooperatively with Information Technology personnel to develop sound information and reporting systems and procedures related to business services.&nbsp; Interpret, apply and explain complex District policies, legal requirements and negotiated agreements.&nbsp; Make effective public presentations.&nbsp; Analyze problems, identify alternative solutions, project consequences of proposed actions and implement recommendations in support of goals.&nbsp; Select, train, lead, direct, supervise and evaluate the performance of assigned personnel.&nbsp; Assure the preparation, maintenance and retention of personnel files and data.&nbsp; Relate effectively to people of varied academic, cultural and socio-economic background using tact, diplomacy and courtesy.&nbsp;&nbsp; Prepare and administer budgets for assigned program areas.&nbsp; Establish and maintain cooperative and effective working relationships with those contacted in the course of work.&nbsp; Communicate clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing.&nbsp; Collect, compile and analyze data. Prepare and present reports and recommendations.&nbsp; Prepare oral and written reports and recommendations.&nbsp; Plan and organize work.&nbsp; Operate modern office equipment such as computer, printer, calculator, copier and facsimile machine.&nbsp; Operate computer and applications software, including database management, spreadsheet, word processing and software related to business services.&nbsp; Exercise initiative and work independently with minimum administrative direction.<br /> <br /> <strong>EDUCATIONAL QUALIFICATIONS: </strong><br /> &bull; Master&rsquo;s degree from an accredited college or university.<br /> &nbsp;<br /> <strong>EXPERIENCE REQUIRED: </strong><br /> &bull; Minimum of one (1) year formal training, internship, or leadership experience in business or public administration or closely related field.<br /> &bull; Five years of increasingly responsible and varied experience in administering a full range District-wide business services management programs, such as accounting, budgeting, payroll, facilities planning/management, purchasing, warehousing, inventory control, in a public sector setting, including at least three years of increasingly responsible experience in negotiating collective bargaining agreements.<br /> &bull; Experience in performing work as described above in a California public education environment is highly desirable.<br /> &nbsp;<br /> <strong>DESIRABLE QUALIFICATIONS</strong><br /> &bull; Earned doctorate degree in business or finance or related field.<br /> &bull; Experience as a Chief Fiscal Officer in a multi-college community college district.<br /> &bull; Experience in managing a budget of $100 million or more.<br /> &bull; Knowledge of integrated business and human resources systems.<br /> &bull; Experience in administering a human resources program in public education.<br /> &bull; Experience in managing capital construction budgets.<br /> &bull; Ability to work effectively in a shared governance environment.<br /> &bull; Ability to work effectively within the state mandate for collective bargaining.<br /> <br /> <br /> <strong>COMPENSATION AND FRINGE BENEFITS:&nbsp; </strong><br /> This position is paid a salary range of&nbsp;
