												<center><strong>TEST ENGINEER<br />Chicago, IL</strong></center> <p><br /><br /><strong>Cardwell Westinghouse, a Wabtec Company</strong>, is one of the largest suppliers in the global rail and transit industries. We are proud of our heritage as an innovator of advanced products and services as well as our use of cutting-edge technologies, lean manufacturing methods and strategic product planning in state-of-the-art workplaces to support our customers' needs. <br /> <br />We currently have an excellent opportunity for an experienced, professional candidate for the position of <strong>Test Engineer</strong>. You will conduct test, record data, calibrate, assemble and / or repair pneumatic, hydraulic, electrical, mechanical, and electromechanical prototypes or existing products for conformance to established design standards. You will document testing in test reports. This position will require some travel. You may also assist in formulating design standards through applied research by performing the following duties.<br /><br /><strong>Essential Duties and Responsibilities include the following. Other duties may be assigned.</strong></p> <li>Plans sequence of testing procedures, determines resources and material requirements for prototypes and / or existing equipment. </li> <li>Apply basic engineering and technical theory to various test situations. </li> <li>Documents research and test data in test reports.</li> <li>Analyzes test data and reports to determine if design meets functional and performance specifications.</li> <li>Sets up standard and special purpose laboratory equipment to test, evaluate, and confirm original design intent. Knowledge and use of a majority of the following test equipment is required: high speed digital data collection systems, instrumentation amplifiers, electronic signal filtering, closed loop hydraulic control systems (MTS 407 preferred), as well as the following transducer types including load cells, LVDTs, pressure transducers, string potentiometers, accelerometers, etc. </li> <li>Soldering, applying strain gages to test specimen, and custom cabling for laboratory and / or field instrumentation applications per layout drawings and schematics is expected.</li> <li>Disassembles prototypes and / or existing equipment to inspect components for wear, distress to, and design defects.</li> <li>Aligns, repairs, replaces, and balances component parts and circuitry.</li> <li>Reassembles prototypes and / or other existing equipment.</li> <li>Grinding, painting, grit blasting, machining (including drilling, milling and lathe work), and use of hand tools for fabrication or repair of prototypes and / or test fixtures.</li> <li>Confers with design and other engineering personnel to suggest practical design modifications as required.</li>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>Qualifications:</strong><br />To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p> <li>B.S. technical college degree, with 5 years experience, an associate (two year) technical college degree or equivalent university engineering technical program certificate with a minimum 7 years experience in an R&amp;D laboratory environment; or 10 to 20 years related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience.</li> <li>Ability to read, analyze, and interpret technical procedures, prints, and assembly instructions. </li> <li>Ability to write and edit test reports and procedure manuals. </li> <li>Effectively present information and respond to questions from management, clients, and customers.</li> <li>Ability to apply basic mathematical relationships as would be necessary to determine high resolution, repeatable, and high accuracy measurements. </li> <li>Ability to use fractions and decimal system mathematical relationships in standard and metric number systems.</li> <li>Ability to use statistical analysis tools to draw conclusions from data generated.</li> <li>Ability to define problems, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions based on observations, test results and collected data. </li> <li>Ability to interpret an extensive variety of technical instructions in mathematical or diagram form and deal with several abstract and concrete variables at the same time.</li> <li>Average skills in Word processing, Spreadsheets, Internet software, and E-mail are required.<br /><br />Cardwell rewards employees with competitive salaries, a comprehensive benefits package and opportunities for career development through training programs, participation in professional organizations and tuition reimbursement. Cardwell is an Equal Opportunity Employer M/F/D/V.<br /><br />For consideration, please forward your resume to <a href="mailto:mmurray@wabtec.com ">mmurray@wabtec.com</a> </li>
