												<p>Kisco Senior Living operates&nbsp;communities in 6 states and employs approximately 1400 associates.We specialize in Independent and Assisted Living and create a sense of community through our philosophy of the Art of Living Well<sup>SM</sup>.</p> <p><strong><em>This position will be home based ideally from North Carolina.</em></strong></p> <p>The Area Nurse is responsible for the design and implementation of Kisco&rsquo;s Policies &amp; Procedures and best practices to support Independent Living, Assisted Living, Alzheimer&rsquo;s Programs, Retirement Home Care and Support Services.&nbsp; Oversees regulatory compliance, provides assistance, support and training to community care service associates.</p> <p><strong>Essential Job Functions:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Ensures clinical compliance with state licensing regulations, organizational and departmental policy and procedures. </li> <li>Works with community and Home Office management teams to research, analyze, and identify potential issues and areas of risk in providing care and services in the communities. </li> <li>&nbsp;Develops and implements plans, processes, and programs to continuously improve the delivery of quality care and services at all Kisco Senior Living communities.&nbsp; Establishes short and long term goals for quality improvement, resident safety and reduction of risk. </li> <li>Participates in the development and delivery of Kisco Policies &amp; Procedures for all care and services including development of documentation, clinical standards, and indicators as requested. </li> <li>Sets standards of performance for care staff and assists in the hiring of all care services staff. </li> <li>&nbsp;Performs community visits to review resident care processes, services and associate adherence to policies and procedures.&nbsp; Documents findings and recommendations. </li> <li>Provides guidance and coaching to care service associates as needed. </li> <li>In collaboration with the Home Office training role, provides training to Assisted Living Directors, care services and other community associates which range from purchased to custom designed training.&nbsp; Training ranges from state required to Kisco specific training. </li> <li>Assists in developing departmental budget and makes recommendations for enhanced revenue and effective use of operational expenses. </li> <li>Assists communities when vacancies occur or additional resources are needed. </li> <li>Stays current on all regulatory requirements and ensures compliance in the communities. </li> <li>Is accountable for knowing and understanding Kisco&rsquo;s Principles, Values, and Beliefs (PVB&rsquo;s) and uses them to guide all actions and decisions. </li> <li>Performs other duties and tasks as assigned or required. </li> </ul>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<div> <p>Ability to maintain confidentiality.&nbsp; Intermediate knowledge of computers, including Microsoft Office software, the Internet and Outlook.&nbsp;Ability to communicate effectively orally and in writing.&nbsp;Ability to deal with confused or demanding residents. Ability to work in a fast-paced, demanding environment with multiple priorities. The ability to interact well with co-workers and function in a team environment.&nbsp;Strong interpersonal, conflict-resolution, analytical, problem-solving and organizational skills. Must have good business judgment.&nbsp;Approaches all encounters with residents, associates, vendors and licensing personnel in a friendly, service-oriented manner.&nbsp;Maintains high standards of personal appearance and grooming. Ability to effectively lead, motivate and manage others. Ability to quickly understand and follow published work rules and procedures. Strong work ethic, including the willingness and ability to work the hours necessary to meet project deadlines.&nbsp; Knowledge of current federal, state and local regulations. Must know how to use general office equipment, including copiers and fax machines. Must be punctual and dependable.</p> </div> <div><strong>Required Experience</strong></div> <div> <p>Nursing degree (RN, BSN, MSN) from accredited college or vocational school is required. &nbsp;A minimum of five years of clinical nursing experience is required of which three years of were spent in a management capacity.&nbsp;Independent Living and/or Assisted Living experience a must.&nbsp;Multi-facility management experience is a plus.</p> <p>Current Registered Nurse license from state of residence and within 90 days of employment must&nbsp;obtain endorsement/reciprocity of Registered Nurse license from all states in which Kisco Senior Living, LLC operates.</p> <p>To review the full job description and to apply please visit:</p> <p><a href="http://www.kiscoseniorliving.com/careers.asp">http://www.kiscoseniorliving.com/careers.asp</a></p> <p>Job tracking code:2010054</p> </div>
