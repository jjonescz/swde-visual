									<div id="jt_job_description">
										
											
												<p>The Staff Assistant - Circulation serves students, faculty, staff and members of the community at the Mott Community College Library in a transaction-intensive, high profile department.&nbsp; The Staff Assistant is the most constant public face of the Mott Community College Library and performs a variety of duties in the delivery of library customer service including materials circulation and basic reference information.&nbsp; The Staff Assistant is responsible for maintaining the PALnet SirsiDynix Symphony automated library system and Datatel accounts and is frequently focused on resolving impediments - overdue materials and other financial obligations - that may hinder a student's ability to register for classes, receive transcripts and grades, and complete college business via the Internet. The Staff Assistant actively contributes to a work environment that is dedicated to the successful operation of the library for users, including a commitment to ongoing professional development, effective communication practices, frequent review of policies and procedures, and a willingness to assist others.</p> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Supervisory Responsibility:</span> <br /></strong></span>No formal supervisory responsibility but, as part of the Circulation team, will assist or participate in the selection, training and monitoring of student workers to include offering input to performance evaluation and performance discussions.</p> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Hours/Schedule:</strong><br /></span>Full time, 40 hours per week/52 weeks per year.&nbsp; Required to work flexible hours as assigned, including some evening or weekend hours. &nbsp;Will be assigned weekend call-in responsibility on a regular basis.&nbsp; Hours at this time are Monday -&nbsp;Friday from 7:45 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.</p> <p>For more information, job description and deadline, please go to <a href="http://www.mott.jobs"><strong>www.mott.jobs</strong></a> to apply online.&nbsp; Mott Community College accepts online applications only.</p>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<ol> <li>Successful completion of courses in library cataloging and reference.&nbsp; In lieu of previously completed coursework, the appropriate, pre-determined courses must be successfully completed within one year of employment start date.&nbsp; (Currently, the appropriate courses, Library Technical Processes (LIB2100) and Library Resources &amp; Services Reference (LIB2170), are offered at Oakland Community College.) </li> <li>One year of experience delivering quality customer service in a setting that required problem solving on customer requests and the ability to exchange information and interface effectively with customers clearly and concisely. </li> <li>Ability to appropriately use and demonstrate proficiency with standard office productivity software and tools, including intermediate level proficiency with MS Word and basic level proficiency with Excel. </li> <li>Proficient computer keyboarding ability and experience. </li> <li>Experience with cashiering, invoicing, or other accounting procedures, using an integrated data information system for business operations. </li> <li>Experience with efficiently organizing, managing and retrieving large amounts of data. </li> <li>Ability to address difficult matters with library users, calmly and fairly. Ability to serve library patrons in a consistently tactful, sensitive, and considerate manner and exhibit a strong identification with the service needs of students, faculty and other library patrons. </li> <li>Ability to handle multiple tasks and projects simultaneously. </li> <li>Excellent communication skills, both orally and in writing. </li> <li>Ability to meet deadlines responsibly. </li> <li>Ability to maintain confidentiality of highly sensitive student and patron records. </li> <li>Ability to work under pressure in a dynamic work environment while adapting to ever changing program demands and priorities. </li> <li>Ability to work independently and with a high level of accuracy. </li> <li>Ability to analyze complex problems and make informed decisions with a minimum of supervision. </li> <li>Ability to communicate and work effectively with a culturally diverse population.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</li> </ol>
