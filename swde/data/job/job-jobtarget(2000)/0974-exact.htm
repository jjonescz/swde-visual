text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">The Institute for Health and Social Policy at McGill University is working to build the first centre of comparative data on health care costs and quality in the world&rsquo;s leading economies.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span><span style="color: black; mso-themecolor: text1;">This initiative will provide information on national investments intended to address social determinants of health, encourage public population health approaches to prevention while also evaluating the cost-effectiveness of medical care.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span></span><span style="color: #0d0d0d; mso-themecolor: text1; mso-themetint: 242;">The goal of this data centre is to markedly enhance our ability to analyze which approaches to population health and health care most effectively combine cost control with high quality health outcomes.</span></span></p> <p style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0pt; text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">&nbsp;</span></p> <p style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0pt; text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: &quot;Arial&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;;">A team of researchers will help build the data centre and carry out a longitudinal analysis of health care costs that is comparative across provinces and countries, looks at historical trends in different sectors within health care, and examines projections for the future. </span></p> <p style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0pt;
