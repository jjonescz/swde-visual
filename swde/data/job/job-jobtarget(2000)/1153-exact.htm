									<div id="jt_job_description">
										
											
												 <p align="center"><strong>Director, Institutional Giving</strong></p> <p align="center"><strong>St. Paul</strong><strong>, </strong><strong>MN</strong></p> <p>Minnesota Public Radio&reg; is one of the nation's premier public radio organizations. With state-of-the-art headquarters in the heart of downtown St. Paul, Minnesota Public Radio operates a regional network of 38 stations, covering Minnesota and parts of Wisconsin, the Dakotas, Michigan, Iowa and Idaho.&nbsp; We are the largest owner and operator of public radio stations nationwide.</p> <p><strong>Position Summary:</strong></p> <p>The Director of Institutional Giving contributes to the income of Minnesota Public Radio | American Public Media by leading grant related development and achieving revenue goals.&nbsp; The position is responsible for leading a development team in identifying and pursuing opportunities for five, six and seven figure grants from foundations, corporations, and government agencies regionally, nationally and extending internationally.&nbsp; Grant proposals encompass unrestricted operating support, restricted project funding, and capital and endowment gifts.&nbsp; The Director will actively establish and foster relationships between American Public Media and the nation's leading philanthropies to build support for advancing the organization's mission.&nbsp; In addition to working externally, this position works closely with the Managing Director of Development, the Senior Vice Presidents of each audience division - Minnesota Public Radio, American Public Media, Classical South Florida, Southern California Public Radio, program producers, project managers, financial analysts, and other cross-functional partners.</p> <p><strong>Position Responsibility:</strong></p> <p>Successfully manage and grow a portfolio of over $6 million in grant revenue from more than 100 different grantors in a fast-paced entrepreneurial nonprofit.&nbsp; This position has significant external responsibilities in addition to leading the internal grant seeking process.</p> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;">External Responsibilities</span></p> <ul> <li> Lead Institutional Giving staff in significantly increasing financial support from relationships with foundations, both regionally and nationally. </li> <li> Lead staff in the creation, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of annual and multi-year grant funding strategies designed to maximize financial support. </li> <li> Broadly communicate the overall MPR|APM strategy for grant funding.</li> <li> Cultivate relationships with major funders and prospects to broaden networks, identify potential funding opportunities, and solicit support.</li> <li> Ensure regular communications, through both written materials and verbal conversations, with funders and prospects.</li> </ul> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Internal Responsibilities</span></p> <ul> <li> Work with content producers and senior management to identify ongoing and potential projects suitable for grant funding.</li> <li> Lead development team in researching and identifying potential funders and creating strategies. </li> <li> Lead the writing and editing of fundable proposals.</li> <li> Facilitate cross-functional teams as necessary for grant development and fulfillment.</li> <li> Provide overall oversight of the grant seeking and grants administration processes.</li> <li> Develop and implement a process and materials for regular communications with funders and prospects.</li> <li> Work synergistically with Major Individual Giving and Underwriting Sales departments to maximize potential for institutional support.</li> <li> Oversee annual budget and forecasting for Institutional Giving department.</li> <li> Work with managers in grant funded areas and their financial analysts to plan role of grant funded proposals in their overall budget and strategic business plans.</li> <li> Work with managers in grant funded areas to ensure alignment between restricted revenue and expenses for grant funded projects. </li> <li> Hire, supervise, train, and manage staff; provide on-going performance feedback; provide annual written review; provide on-going coaching and support; and develop individualized development plans.</li> <li> Establish annual and multi-year goals for individual staff members. </li> <li> Foster a creative and productive work environment. Create development opportunities for staff and training to enhance skills and foster growth.</li> </ul> 
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>Required Education and Experience:</strong></p> <ul> <li> Bachelor's degree required -- English, Journalism, Business, Media, Communications or related liberal arts field preferred.</li> <li> Minimum eight (8) years development experience in securing significant grants from corporations, foundations and/or government institutions.</li> <li> Demonstrated track record in producing excellent written materials and successful solicitations, resulting in consistently winning six figure-plus grants.</li> <li> Previous experience in researching and identifying major new funding prospects.</li> <li> Proven success in building relationships with prospective funders resulting in funding support.</li> <li> Significant financial and budget management experience and skills.</li> <li> Prior experience managing a talented group or development department is required. </li> <li> Management experience must demonstrate an ability to recruit and develop staff with high standards, demonstrating an ability to coach, inspire and lead. </li> </ul> <p><strong>Required Skills, Knowledge and Abilities: </strong></p> <ul> <li> Excellent demonstrated writing and editing skills.</li> <li> Excellent verbal communication skills.</li> <li> Leadership qualities of presence, confidence, judgment and integrity are required to command the respect and response of foundation personnel, colleagues and senior executive personnel.</li> <li> Knowledge of the foundation community, current trends and staff, and their interests and guidelines.</li> <li> Ability to develop and communicate strategies.</li> <li> Ability to prepare and make presentations.</li> <li> Must be capable of quickly synthesizing complex ideas and activities for coherent and compelling major grant projects in radio production, journalism, arts, outreach, Internet and more.</li> <li> Motivated, creative, and organized self-starter.</li> <li> Team player with demonstrated ability to collaborate to accomplish goals.</li> <li> Able to handle multiple projects and conflicting priorities.</li> <li> Comfort with ambiguity and constant change.</li> <li> Ability to recruit, motivate, lead, coach, inspire, manage and retain talented development staff.</li> <li> Must know how to build grant timelines and budgets.</li> <li> Working knowledge of Windows-based software applications<strong>.</strong></li> <li> Available to travel frequently to meet funders and prospects.</li> <li> Committed to and enthusiastic about the mission and programs and services of MPR|APM.</li> </ul> <p><strong>Preferred Skills and Experience:</strong></p> <ul> <li> Master's degree preferred - Development, Public Administration, English, Journalism, Business, Media, Communications or related field desirable.</li> <li> CRFE or ACFRE designation is preferred.</li> <li> Prior experience working with major national foundations in the arts and culture field.</li> </ul> <p><strong>Reporting To This Position:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </strong></p> <ul> <li> 2 Senior Development Officers</li> <li> Development Coordinator</li> <li> Development Assistant</li> </ul> <p align="left">&nbsp;</p> <p align="center">To apply, email a resume and cover letter to careers@americanpublicmedia.org.</p> <p
