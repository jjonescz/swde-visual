									<div id="jt_job_description">
										
											
												<p>Max Planck Institute for Software Systems (MPI-SWS)</p> <p>Tenure-track openings</p> <p>Applications are invited for tenure-track and tenured faculty<br />positions in all areas related to the study, design, and engineering<br />of software systems.&nbsp; These areas include, but are not limited to,<br />data and information management, programming systems, software<br />verification, parallel, distributed and networked systems, and<br />embedded systems, as well as cross-cutting areas like security,<br />machine learning, usability, and social aspects of software systems.<br />A doctoral degree in computer science or related areas and an<br />outstanding research record are required.&nbsp; Successful candidates<br />are expected to build a team and pursue a highly visible research<br />agenda, both independently and in collaboration with other groups. <br />Senior candidates must have demonstrated leadership abilities and<br />recognized international stature.</p> <p>MPI-SWS, founded in 2005, is part of a network of eighty Max Planck<br />Institutes, Germany's premier basic research facilities.&nbsp; MPIs have an<br />established record of world-class, foundational research in the fields<br />of medicine, biology, chemistry, physics, technology and humanities.<br />Since 1948, MPI researchers have won 17 Nobel prizes.&nbsp; MPI-SWS aspires<br />to meet the highest standards of excellence and international<br />recognition with its research in software systems.</p> <p>To this end, the institute offers a unique environment that combines<br />the best aspects of a university department and a research laboratory:</p> <p>a) Faculty receive generous base funding to build and lead a team of<br />graduate students and post-docs. They have full academic freedom and<br />publish their research results freely.</p> <p>b) Faculty supervise doctoral theses, and have the opportunity to teach<br />graduate and undergraduate courses.</p> <p>c) Faculty are provided with outstanding technical and administrative<br />support facilities as well as internationally competitive compensation<br />packages.</p> <p>MPI-SWS currently has 8 tenured and tenure-track faculty, and <br />is funded to support 17 faculty and about 100 doctoral and<br />post-doctoral positions. Additional growth through outside funding is<br />possible. We maintain an open, international and diverse work<br />environment and seek applications from outstanding researchers<br />regardless of national origin or citizenship. The working language is<br />English; knowledge of the German language is not required for a<br />successful career at the institute.</p> <p>The institute is located in Kaiserslautern and Saarbruecken, in the<br />tri-border area of Germany, France and Luxembourg. The area offers a<br />high standard of living, beautiful surroundings and easy access to<br />major metropolitan areas in the center of Europe, as well as a<br />stimulating, competitive and collaborative work environment. In<br />immediate proximity are the MPI for Informatics, Saarland University,<br />the Technical University of Kaiserslautern, the German Center for<br />Artificial Intelligence (DFKI), and the Fraunhofer Institutes for<br />Experimental Software Engineering and for Industrial Mathematics.</p> <p>Qualified candidates should apply online at<br /><a href="http://www.mpi-sws.org/application">http://www.mpi-sws.org/application</a>.&nbsp; The review of applications will<br />begin on January 3, 2011, and applicants are strongly encouraged to<br />apply by that date; however, applications will continue to be accepted<br />through January 2011.</p> <p>The institute is committed to increasing the representation of<br />minorities, women and individuals with physical disabilities in<br />Computer Science. We particularly encourage such individuals to apply.</p>
