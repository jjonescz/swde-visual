												<p><span>&nbsp; <div><strong>Position Summary</strong></div> <div> <div>Teach For America seeks a managing director of student achievement measurement to lead the team which is designing and implementing the next generation of our student achievement metrics and goals. These metrics and goals are the systems we use to convert the results of the assessments administered by our corps members into actionable student achievement data at every level of our system, and they comprise the most critical (and most complex) set of outcomes data we track as an organization. We are currently engaging in a major redesign of these metrics and goals so that they are better calibrated across the range of contexts faced by our corps members (subjects, grades, and student starting points) and they better reflect the impact of corps members across the entire performance continuum. The managing director of student achievement measurement will lead all aspects of the redesign: framing the critical design choices and their implications, facilitating the decision-making process with regard to these choices, overseeing the construction of the metrics and goals themselves through data collection and analysis, translating the design into concrete policies, and working with others in the organization to test and implement the new system (including design of data systems, corps member/staff training, and communication plans). This individual will work directly with the vice president of strategy and research (and other senior leaders throughout the organization) and will directly manage a small team focused on the analytical component of this work. Experience with student achievement data systems, econometrics/statistics, and/or other highly quantitative performance management systems are all major assets for the role.</div> &nbsp;</div> <div><strong>About Teach For America</strong></div> <div>Teach For America is the national corps of top recent college graduates who commit to teach for at least two years in urban and rural public schools and become lifelong leaders in the effort to expand educational opportunity. Our mission is to build the movement to eliminate educational inequity by enlisting the nation's most promising future leaders in the effort.</div> <div>&nbsp;</div> <div>In the 2010-2011 school year, more than 8,200 first- and second-year Teach For America corps members will be teaching in 39 regions across the United States. Since 1990, Teach For America corps members have reached more than 3 million students.&nbsp; More than 20,000 Teach For America alumni are working in education and many other fields, where they continue to advocate for students and families in low-income communities. While only one in six corps members say they were interested in the teaching profession before joining Teach For America, nearly two-thirds of our alumni remain in the field of education.&nbsp;</div> <div>&nbsp;</div> <div>We are a high-growth, outcomes-oriented organization, with a $220 million budget and over 1.500 staff. We operate in an entrepreneurial environment, maintain focus on quantitative measures, and are committed to continuous improvement.</div> <div>&nbsp;</div> <div><strong>Team Overview</strong></div> <div>The Teacher Preparation, Support, and Development teams work to ensure that new teachers have the initial training and ongoing support necessary to make academic gains and remain lifelong stewards of the movement. The team develops teacher training and staff professional development frameworks, methods, and materials for our pre-service institute and our two-year ongoing training and support program in regions, and drives innovation and consistency in how teachers collect and assess data in order to achieve academic gains with students.</div> <div>&nbsp;</div> <div><strong>Responsibilities</strong></div> <ul> <li>Defining the vision and strategy for the organization's student achievement metrics and goals system: aspirations, goals, sequencing and prioritization of major initiatives, etc.</li> <li>Serving as the primary thought leader for all elements of the design of the next-generation student achievement metrics and goals system</li> <li>Overseeing the construction of the new system: gathering and analyzing student achievement data across a wide variety of assessments to produce benchmarks, designing data gathering and tracking systems, etc.</li> <li>Translating system design into concrete policies at every level of the organization</li> <li>Partnering with others across the organization to plan for and roll out nationwide testing and implementation of the new student achievement metrics and goals system: project planning, pilot design and execution, roll-out plan, change management, etc.</li> <li>Leading the design of tools and systems for corps members and staff to interact with the new metrics and goals</li> <li>Conducting student achievement analysis as we evaluate and enhance the measurement system and for broader Teach For America strategic and operational planning at all levels of the organization</li> <li>Representing the student achievement metrics and goals system internally and ensuring effective communication at all levels of the organization</li> <li>Representing the student achievement metrics and goals system externally and building strong collaborations with critical partners (e.g., state departments of education, school districts, charter management organization, testing companies, etc.)</li> <li>Building and developing a high-performing student achievement measurement system (SAMS) analytics team</li> </ul>
