												<p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: Times New Roman;">The AIDS and Cancer Virus Program (ACVP), SAIC-Frederick, Inc., pursues multidisciplinary research programs in basic and applied molecular virology, viral immunology, and retroviral pathogenesis. Will work on a project investigating transmission of Kaposi&rsquo;s Sarcoma Herpes Virus (KSHV) in a mother and child cohort in Uganda. The position will be based at SAIC-Frederick, but will require extended placement at the Uganda Virus Research Institute, Entebbe, Uganda. The project includes development of assays to measure cellular immune responses to KSHV and the transfer of such assays to Uganda. The position will also include training technical staff and students in Uganda in molecular and immunological assays including real time PCR and ELISAs.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size: small; font-family: Times New Roman;"> <p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">Excellent salary and benefits accompany this position.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>For immediate consideration, please apply online for Research Associate II<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>#184368 at http://saic-frederick.com</p> <p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;">&nbsp;SAIC is an equal opportunity employer and values cultural diversity in the workplace.</p> </span></p>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p style="margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-size: small; font-family: Times New Roman;">Bachelor&rsquo;s degree from an accredited college/university in a field related to biomedical research or four (4) years equivalent experience in the appropriate field in lieu of degree. Foreign educated candidates who have completed part or all of their education outside of the United States must have their foreign education evaluated by and SAIC-approved accrediting organization to assure that it has met the equivalency of the qualifications of degree work in the United States. In addition to educational requirements, a minimum of five (5) years related biomedical experience. Must have experience with ELISPOT, FACS analysis, real time PCR, basic molecular skills, ELISA, experience working with clinical samples, familiarity with laboratory analysis software. Must be able to spend between 6-9 months of the year in Uganda (at approximately 3 month intervals). This position is subject to obtaining a Public Trust Clearance.</span></p>
