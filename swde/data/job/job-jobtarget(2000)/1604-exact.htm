												<h1><span style="font-size: small;">PURPOSE OF CLASSIFICATION</span></h1> <p>The purpose of this classification is to perform technical tasks on an analytical level in conducting initial and final plans reviews for all grading and development permit applications ensuring compliance with City codes and standards.&nbsp; Reviews and analyzes site plans for impact on adjacent developments, infrastructure layout, utility design, grading and drainage improvements, roadway requirements, erosion and sediment control and stream buffer standards.</p> <h1><span style="font-size: small;">ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS</span></h1> <p>Performs various reviews of plans and applications for compliance and completeness; reviews erosion control plans for compliance with established guidelines as stated in the Manual for Erosion and Sediment Control in Georgia; coordinates review of all department plans with departmental representatives; monitors status of all development plan reviews with all departments (main focus); reviews applications and approves land disturbance permits for single family dwellings;&nbsp; reviews all plans for compliance with all stream buffer setbacks.</p> <p>Participates in various engineering related duties and communicates with internal and outside engineering representatives; reviews and analyzes site plans for impact on adjacent developments, infrastructure layout, utility design, grading and drainage improvements, roadway requirements, erosion and sediment control and stream buffer standards; coordinates development plan redline corrections with outside consulting engineers; reviews civil plans for engineering and code compliance; represents the engineering department in various meetings as necessary; submits maintains and controls all engineering archive files.</p> <p>Performs routine and non-routine administrative tasks in support of departmental operations; calculates fee parameters for development permits; prepares correspondence, reports and related documents.</p> <p>Communicates with and responds to various groups and individuals; receives and refers public erosion control complaints and inquiries to inspectors who interact with consulting engineering firms to prepare development plans and land disturbances permits; interacts with other City department representatives; coordinates and conducts Development Plan Review Team (DRPT) meetings; establishes meeting agenda and meeting schedule.</p> <p>Operates a personal computer, printer, calculator, copier, fax machine, phone system, and other equipment as necessary to complete essential functions, to include the use of word processing, spreadsheet, database, and other system software utilized within the department.</p> <p><span style="font-size: small;"><strong>PERFORMANCE APTITUDES</strong></span></p> <p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Specific Knowledge, Skills, or Abilities</span></strong>:&nbsp; Must have the ability to use practical applications involving codes and zoning ordinances.&nbsp; Must have thorough understanding of departmental operations and functions, including the principles and practices of land planning, development, surveying and plan preparation.</p> <p>Must have the ability to review, understand and interpret design drawings, site plans, construction specifications, maps, and plats.&nbsp; Must be highly organized for inputting and recall.</p> <p>&nbsp;<strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Data Utilization</span></strong>:&nbsp; Requires the ability to review, classify, categorize, prioritize, and/or analyze data.&nbsp;
