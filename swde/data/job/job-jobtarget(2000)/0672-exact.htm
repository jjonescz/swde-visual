									<div id="jt_job_description">
										
											
												<p>Providence College and Seminary invites applications for the position of&nbsp;Vice&nbsp;President for&nbsp;Finance.&nbsp;The&nbsp;Vice&nbsp;President&nbsp;serves as the Chief Financial Officer of the Institution and supervises&nbsp;the business office.&nbsp;Providence is a Christian academic community in the evangelical tradition.&nbsp;Candidates will agree with the Providence Covenant of Faith found on the About Us page at <a href="http://www.providencecollege.ca" target="_blank">www.providencecollege.ca</a> or <a href="http://www.providenceseminary.ca" target="_blank">www.providenceseminary.ca</a>.</p> <p>In addition to appropriate training and an accounting designation (CA, CGA, CMA), the successful candidate will have significant experience in corporate non-profit finance.&nbsp;He/she will have a history of positive working relationships, clear communication, and professional conduct. &nbsp;The Vice President is a member of the President's Cabinet and has a significant role in the overall development of the Institution.&nbsp;All candidates will be considered, however Canadians and permanent residents of Canada will be given priority.&nbsp;A resume may be sent by&nbsp;<strong>December 15&nbsp;</strong>to:</p> <blockquote>David H. Johnson, Executive Vice President<br />Providence College <br />10 College Crescent<br />Otterburne, Manitoba R0A 1G0<br />Phone: 1-204-433-7488, ext. 266<br />Fax: 1-204-433-7158<br /></blockquote>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><strong>General Summary:<br /></strong>-&nbsp;The VP for Finance is an active member of the President&rsquo;s Cabinet and is responsible for the development and implementation of short and long-term strategic planning with that group.&nbsp; To support these responsibilities the person in this position must have excellent communication skills and a proactive approach.<br />-&nbsp;The VP for Finance is responsible for all budgeting and financial reporting.</p> <p><br /><strong>Essential Duties and Responsibilities:<br /></strong>-&nbsp;Oversees the preparation of the annual budget and exercises general budgetary control over all accounts.<br />-&nbsp;Supervises proper record-keeping and accounting of all financial transactions of the Institution. <br />-&nbsp;Represents Cabinet on the Audit &amp; Operations Committee of the Board<br />-&nbsp;Schedules and facilitates the annual audit with the audit firm and Audit Committee.&nbsp; <br />-&nbsp;Provides regular reports on the status of the Institution&rsquo;s finances to the President, President&rsquo;s Cabinet, Board and others.<br />-&nbsp;Negotiates loans, bank fees and other banking arrangements as required.<br />-&nbsp;Is familiar with GST, PST and all charitable reporting required for the organization.<br />-&nbsp;Ensures provision of adequate and effective business service to the PC/PTS employees in areas such as purchasing, printing, banking, telephone and data processing.<br />-&nbsp;Is familiar with all financial aspects of the Blackbaud enterprise software.<br />-&nbsp;Supervises all salary and payroll procedures, as well as the financial arrangements with respect to employee benefit programs.<br />-&nbsp;Reconciles and analyzes balance sheet and income statement accounts including preparation of associated journal entries and working papers.<br />-&nbsp;Prepares divisional and organizational financial statements or reports<br />-&nbsp;Compiles, analyzes and interprets a variety of financial and transactional information to review and improve business and accounting processes as well as system setup.<br />-&nbsp;Prepares working papers and other associated documents for year-end and various audit requirements.<br />-&nbsp;Reviews and verifies accounting entries and adjustments to help maintain the integrity of accounting and reporting systems.<br />-&nbsp;Develops and produces routine and ad hoc management information reports for a varied audience.<br />-&nbsp;Provides a variety of business support, project assistance and analysis for various Departments.<br />-&nbsp;Prepares and remits reports to various governmental and non-governmental agencies such as Canada Revenue Agency, Statistics Canada, Canadian Council for Christian Charities, Association of Theological Schools, Association of Biblical Higher Education, and others as required.<br />-&nbsp;Assists other departmental employees with advice, training, and expertise on the use of financial systems.</p> <p><strong>Job Specifications/Qualifications: <br /></strong>-&nbsp;Spiritual qualifications should include references which indicate this person has a mature, consistent walk with the Lord, and has had an established fellowship with a local body of believers.&nbsp; Candidate must read and sign complete agreement with the Doctrinal Statement and be able to give a clear, verbal testimony of their personal faith in Jesus Christ as their personal Saviour.<br />-&nbsp;Canadian Professional Accounting Designation (CA, CGA, CMA)<br />-&nbsp;Appropriate administrative experience.&nbsp; This person should present him/herself in an executive image in both appearance and conversation.<br />-&nbsp;Five years recent related progressive accounting experience in a computerized environment including experience in developing spreadsheets, financial statements, analytical reports as well as general accounting responsibilities.<br />-&nbsp;Job requires sitting at desk for long periods of time; general office conditions.<br />-&nbsp;May include competency requirements in the areas of knowledge, skill, ability, or characteristics associated with high performance on a job.</p> <p><strong>Skills/Knowledge:<br /></strong>-&nbsp;Knowledge of the Corporation's business environment and mandate. <br />-&nbsp;Knowledge and understanding of corporate systems.&nbsp; Ability to use automated data systems effectively and to contribute to their design and improvement. <br />-&nbsp;Exceptional analytical skills and apply them through deductive reasoning to both empirical evidence to the statistical output of controlled data systems. <br />-&nbsp;Deal with highly sensitive files, impacting upon the credibility of both the Corporation and the federal government.&nbsp; Sense of diplomacy and tact in working in sensitive and demanding areas. <br />-&nbsp;Ability to take initiative and work independently on policy and product improvement. <br />-&nbsp;Organization skills to manage several ongoing and competing tasks at one time. <br />-&nbsp;Ability to work in a multi-disciplinary team environment. <br />-&nbsp;Above average writing and communication skills are a prerequisite.</p>
