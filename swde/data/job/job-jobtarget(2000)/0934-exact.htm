												<b>Location</b>: MGH Main Campus - Boston<br /><br /><b>Department</b>: Neurosurgery<br /><b>Regular/Temporary</b>: Regular<br /><b>Standard Hours</b>: 40.00<br /><br /><b>Full Time/Part Time</b>: Full-Time<br /><b>Job ID</b>: 2208189<br /><br /><b>Description</b>: <span class='PSLONGEDITBOX' >Under general supervision, coordinates and takes a lead role for performing a variety of technical support and maintenance functions for the Department of Neurosurgery in the operating room environment. She/he will be responsible for the comprehensive technical support of highly sophisticated biomedical instrumentation systems and equipment used in Neurosurgery (incl. stereotactic equipment, intraoperative MRI, frameless stereotaxy, various current and future digital-imaging systems). These systems are complex, computerized electronics needing technical support that extend well beyond maintenance and repair. Works independently and in collaboration with the neurosurgical faculty, and industry representatives to expedite the surgical schedule and further develop the use of frameless stereotaxy in endoscopic, spinal, vascular and functional neurosurgery.&nbsp; Reports to the Chief of Neurosurgery.<br /><br /><br /><br />PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:Indicate key areas of responsibility, major job duties, special projects and key objectives for this position.&nbsp; These items should be evaluated throughout the year and included in the written annual evaluation.<br /><br />Responsible for the care, maintenance and operation of biomedical instrumentation systems and equipment used by neurosurgery, including (but not limited to) the following:<br /><br />Frameless stereotaxy equipment<br />Framebased equipment<br />Intraoperative MR equipment&nbsp; <br />Computer guided endoscopic equipment<br />Skull base neurosurgery equipment<br />Neurovascular surgery equipment<br />Transphenoidal equipment (particularly in the intraoperative MRI environment)<br />Neurosurgical spinal equipment<br />Operative neurosurgical microscopes<br />Still and video digital equipment<br /><br />Troubleshoots these digital, video and computer related systems, particularly preoperatively and during surgeries and assists neurosurgeons in utilization and editing.<br /><br />Develops and manages schedule of preventative maintenance, establishing maintenance protocols as necessary.&nbsp; Reviews maintenance protocols developed by manufacturers and biomedical engineering.<br /><br />Performs regularly scheduled and emergency maintenance on all biomedical instrumentation equipment used by neurosurgery as noted above.<br /><br />Calibrates and troubleshoots neurosurgery equipment and biomedical instrumentation systems to assure they operate properly and are in compliance with all applicable codes and standards.&nbsp; Seeks ways to reduce breakdowns, eliminate safety hazards, and enhance accuracy and reliability.&nbsp; Refers irresolvable technical problems to biomedical engineering only if the item is covered by warranty, by a service contract, or needs to be returned to the manufacturer for repair.<br /><br />Evaluates the equipment performance to ensure the validity and efficiency of operation by determining and utilizing appropriate test setup.&nbsp; He/she may use conventional Standard Test Equipment, High Level Test Equipment and/or develop a technique or unconventional application for the equipment being reviewed.<br /><br />May assist in designing, developing, and adapting equipment for a specific patient need or research.<br /><br />Creates, designs and maintains various relational database and spreadsheet applications.&nbsp; Downloads data from Hospital mainframe systems and interacts with Information Resources personnel.<br /><br />Responsible for preoperatively loading radiological images into the navigation systems and setting up for use in the OR suite.<br /><br />Trains and provides on-going updates to physicians and nurses in correct equipment usage and operation.<br /><br />Assists in performing needs assessment, financial analyses, productivity analyses as they relate to the procurement of biomedical devices, imaging equipment, and software for clinical use.<br /><br />Determines equipment and supply needs and notifies procurement personnel to order.<br /><br />Interacts regularly with physicians, nurses, technicians, support personnel, administrative personnel, and manufacturer representatives.<br /><br /><br />QUALIFICATIONS:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (MUST be realistic, neither overstated nor understated, and related to the essential functions of the job.)<br /><br />Associates Degree with 3-5 years of prior related experience or Bachelor Degree and 2+ years of prior related experience required.<br /><br />Prior training and/or experience with intraoperative computer-oriented systems and digital imaging systems. <br /><br />Creativity in using this equipment in the operating room setting is important.<br /><br />SKILLS/ ABILITIES/ COMPETENCIES REQUIRED:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; (MUST be realistic, measurable, objective, and related to the essential functions of the job.)<br /><br />Requires independent judgment.<br /><br />Ability to work well under pressure.<br /><br />Available to be &quot;on call&quot; evenings and weekends.<br /><br />Excellent organizational and communication skills.<br /><br />Knowledge of medical terminology.<br /><br />Knowledge of optics, mechanics, computers and electrical safety for application to difficult problems in the high sophisticated biomedical instrumentation systems and equipment used by neurosurgery.<br /><br />Knowledge of operating room procedures and priorities.<br /><br />Ability to work quickly and expertly in assistance to neurosurgeons and nursing staff. <br /><br />Ability to interpret and use regulatory agency guidelines and standards, equipment maintenance guidelines, and service manuals.<br /><br />Proficiency in both IBM PC (Microsoft 95, 97, 2000, NT, and XP) and Apple computer systems. <br />WORKING CONDITIONS:Describe the conditions in which the work is performed.<br /><br />Works primarily in the operating room and adjacent areas usually under sterile conditions. Special gloves, gowns and masks are required as standard apparel.<br /><br />SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITY:&nbsp; List the number of FTEs supervised.<br /><br />The incumbent independently plans and carries out duties as they relate to design, modification, repair and/or testing of systems and equipment. No direct reports.<br /><br />FISCAL RESPONSIBILITY:Indicate financial scope information, i.e.: size of budget, volume, revnue, etc.<br /><br />None.</span>
