												<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Position Summary</strong></span><br />The Director of Public Relations is responsible for strategy, planning and execution of public relations campaigns, communications programs, and initiatives to increase public understanding of and support for the Museum&rsquo;s mission and goals. This position requires a skilled writer and communicator able to work successfully with the media as well as a variety of stakeholder groups and create presentations for a wide range of internal and external audiences. In addition the position is responsible for proactively developing strategic opportunities for telling the Crystal Bridges story at top-tier venues. The Director of Public Relations works in collaboration with administrators, staff, volunteers and trustees. The Director of Public Relations reports directly to the Deputy Director of Museum Relations.</p> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Principal Responsibilities (Essential Functions)</strong></span></p> <ul> <li>Co-direct message platforms and communication channel strategies for internal, external, and online audiences with the Director of Marketing.</li> <li>Collaboratively create, manage and implement media and public relations campaign strategies for Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art.</li> <li>Generate high visibility in local, state, regional and national media for Museum&rsquo;s program, services and facilities.</li> <li>Develop public information programs to generate understanding of and support for the mission and values of the Museum.</li> <li>Serve as first point of contact for the media and lead spokesperson for Museum.</li> <li>Act as media relations liaison during press previews and visits.</li> <li>Articulate key Museum issues and positions in a range of communication channels.</li> <li>Manage on-the-ground logistics and provide advance staffing at PR events and presentations. Brief speakers on audience, venue logistics, tone and delivery style.</li> <li>Provide editorial counsel and support for all print and electronic communications.</li> <li>Develop and maintain the standards for writing including tone, voice, and style.</li> <li>Develop and maintain departmental budget.</li> <li>Manage work with public relations agencies, when necessary.</li> <li>Create and assess metrics to gauge and evaluate the effectiveness and impact of media activities and prepare routine reports that detail these activities.</li> <li>Work collaboratively with Museum Relations leadership and department managers to deliver excellent service to the Museum staff, members and guests.</li> </ul>
											
										
										
										
											<br><b>Job Requirements</b><br>
											
												<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Minimum Qualifications</strong></span></p> <p><strong>Education, Training, and Traits:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Bachelor&rsquo;s degree effective in fast-paced working environments with absolute deadlines.</li> <li>Superb writing and editing skills and a strong sense of narrative and story-telling.</li> <li>Excellent verbal communication skills and the ability to communicate in a clear and persuasive manner.</li> <li>A seasoned, confident communications professional with a good sense of judgment who has counseled senior executives and earned their trust.</li> <li>Excellent PowerPoint skills and the ability to create compelling graphics, videos and other visuals to help tell a story.</li> <li>Experience in web content production and writing.</li> <li>Ability to understand and maintain the highest levels of confidentiality</li> <li>Highest ethics as they relate to all aspects of museum practices</li> <li>A high level of professional integrity and the ability to handle sensitive issues and situations with discretion.</li> <li>Knowledge of museum operations, the arts industry, and arts marketing is a plus.</li> <li>Belief in the Museum as a cause for social good and a positive, dynamic force for economic development</li> </ul> <p><strong>Work Experience: </strong></p> <ul> <li>Ten or more years direct experience as a public relations manager</li> <li>Work experience in at least two of the following areas: media relations, strategic planning, marketing, journalism</li> <li>Demonstrated experience using the Internet as a public relations tool</li> <li>Agency experience and/or management preferred.</li> <li>Well-developed contacts with national and local editors and journalists in electronic and print media is preferred.</li> </ul> <p><br /><em>In regard to education and experience, an equivalent combination of relevant education and/or experience will satisfy the minimum requirements. </em></p> <p><strong>Licenses and Certifications:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Valid driver&rsquo;s license</li> </ul> <p><strong>Skills and Abilities:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Highly effective written, verbal, and listening communication skills and a demonstrated ability to communicate clearly and professionally.</li> <li>Highly developed public speaking skills</li> <li>Demonstrated organizational planning, problem-solving and collaboration skills</li> <li>Demonstrated understanding and use of diplomacy</li> <li>Proficient computer skills in MS Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Database and media relations software (Vocus) knowledge preferred.</li> </ul> <p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Physical Demands and Work Environment:</strong></span><br />The physical demands and work environment characteristics described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this position. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.</p> <ul> <li><em>Physical demands:</em> While performing the duties of this job, the employee is required to travel independently (approximately 25% of time) within museum office and spaces, construction site and in the communities served. In the work environments described below, position requires verbal and written conversation with others, sitting, standing, walking, reaching, lifting/moving objects up to 25 pounds, and use of hands to finger, handle, or feel objects, tools, or controls. The position occasionally requires standing, walking, and speaking for an extended period of time. Vision abilities required by the job include close vision.</li> <li><em>Work Environment: </em>Work will be performed in an office environment, museum spaces, construction site, and in the communities served.
