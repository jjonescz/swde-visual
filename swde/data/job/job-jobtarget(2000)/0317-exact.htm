We have experienced tremendous growth and success, due in large part to an innovative lineup of residential and commercial communications products and services, and an unsurpassed commitment to excellent customer service. To support our continuing efforts, we are currently seeking an experienced Maintenance Technician.</a></p> <p> <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0"> <tbody> <tr> <td width="535" valign="top"> </td> </tr> <tr> <td width="535" valign="top"> <p>SUMMARY</p> <p>This position is responsible for preventative maintenance, troubleshooting, alignment, testing and repair of the HFC plant.&nbsp; Responsibilities to support all services including analog, digital, fiber and other communications mediums. Required to work on-call.</p> <p>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES&nbsp;include the following. Other duties may be assigned.&nbsp;</p> <ul> <li>1. Performs system sweeping, alignment, testing, troubleshooting and repair of the HFC RF two way plant.</li> <li>2. Produces and maintains documentation and records of all activities and testing as required.</li> <li>3. Participates in regularly scheduled and emergency on-call duty beyond normal working hours as required. Position may require non-standard working hours on a regular basis in order to perform duties at hours that are least disruptive to customers.</li> <li>4. Maintains thorough and current proficiency of FCC performance specifications and can, without supervision initiate and complete comprehensive testing including (but not limited to) all FCC required or recommended tests and measurements.</li> <li>5. Utilize computer software and test equipment for variety of measurements including: RF levels, signal leakage, signal to noise, carrier to noise, low frequency disturbances and composite triple beat. </li> <li>6. Perform proactive and reactive monitoring and maintenance of HFC power Supplies.</li> <li>7. Perform general construction duties to include splicing and activation of new cable plant and emergency restoration of the communication network.</li> <li>8. Maintains all equipment assigned in proper working order including regular calibration, repair, adjustment and alignment. </li> <li>9. Test and maintain backup powering systems- including generators, uninterruptible power supplies, and temporary feeds.</li> <li>10. Utilize optical time domain reflectometer to perform splice acceptance tests as well as fault location.</li> <li>11. Proficient with software programs for storage and archival of test equipment results.</li> <li>12. Performs maintenance by climbing on ladders, power/telephone poles and other structures, lifting and carrying up to 75 pounds, digging, crawling, stooping and standing. Works outside throughout the year. Works in various weather conditions, which may be extreme hot or cold conditions.</li> <li>13.
