Naval Architect&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>DUTY LOCATION</strong>: 1 vacancy</p> <p><strong>SERIES &amp; GRADE</strong>:&nbsp; GS-0871-05/07 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Washington, DC&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p> <p><strong>SALARY</strong>:&nbsp; $&nbsp; 34,075 - $54,4875&nbsp; ANNUALLY&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p> <p><strong>PROMOTION POTENTIAL</strong>:&nbsp; GS-13</p> <p><strong><em>NOTE:&nbsp; This is an excepted service Federal Career Intern position.&nbsp; The candidate will serve in the excepted service for a period not to exceed two (2) years under a formal training plan.&nbsp; Upon successful completion of the intern position the candidate may be non-competitively converted to a permanent career or career-conditional appointment.</em></strong></p> <p><strong>POSITION LOCATION:</strong>&nbsp; Naval Architecture Division, Office of Design and Engineering Standards, U.S. Coast Guard, Department of Homeland Security</p> <p><strong>WHO MAY APPLY</strong>:&nbsp; Open to all U.S. Citizens</p> <p><strong>SUMMARY OF DUTIES</strong>:&nbsp; The incumbent of this position serves as a naval architecture <br />specialist in developing national and international regulations and standards that govern the safe design, construction, and operation of commercial vessels in the areas of hull structure, stability and load lines.&nbsp;<br /><br />The incumbent also performs the following duties: <br />- &nbsp;Provides policy guidance and technical clarifications in the areas of hull structure, stability and load lines, and resolves appeals regarding the same topics;<br />- &nbsp;Engages with various National and International standards committees, as well as classification society rules committees, regarding commercial vessel hull structure topics; <br />- &nbsp;Assists the Executive Director of the Ship Structure Committee, an interagency research and development committee for safer ship structures;<br />- &nbsp;Advises the United States delegation to the International Maritime Organization's Sub-Committee on Stability and Load Lines and on Fishing Vessels Safety (SLF); and <br />- &nbsp;Occasionally (approximately 2-3 times per year) travels to perform the duties described above.</p> <p>Duties and responsibilities at lower grade levels will be performed with higher levels of supervision.</p> <p>More information regarding the Naval Architecture Division can be found at the following link:</p> <p><a href="http://www.uscg.mil/hq/cg5/cg5212/">http://www.uscg.mil/hq/cg5/cg5212/</a></p> <p>Please go to the link below for detailed Federal Qualification Standards for this position.</p> <p><a href="http://www.opm.gov/qualifications/Standards/group-stds/gs-prof.asp">http://www.opm.gov/qualifications/Standards/group-stds/gs-prof.asp</a></p> <p><strong>SPECIAL FACTORS PERTINENT TO THIS POSITION</strong>:</p> <ul> <li>U.S.&nbsp; Citizenship required.</li> <li>Relocation expenses MAY be paid</li> </ul> <p><strong>HOW TO APPLY FOR THIS POSITION</strong>:</p> <p>You may submit your current SF-171 (Application for Federal Employment), Resume, or an</p> <p>OF-612 (Optional Application for Federal Employment).</p> <p>&nbsp;You may choose which form to submit provided it contains all required information.</p> <p>Your application must contain the following:</p> <p><strong>Personal Information:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Full name</li> <li>Mailing address (with zip code)</li> <li>Country of citizenship (this job requires United States citizenship)</li> </ul> <p><strong>Education:</strong></p> <ul> <li>High School name, city, state, and date of diploma or GED</li> <li>College/University names, city, and state</li> <li>Majors</li> <li>Type and year of degree(s) received</li> <li>Overall GPA</li> </ul> <p><strong>Work Experience (paid and unpaid):</strong></p> <ul> <li>Job Title</li> <li>Duties and accomplishments</li> <li>Employer's name and address</li> <li>Supervisor's name and telephone number</li> <li>Starting and ending dates (month and year)</li> <li>Hours per week</li> <li>Salary and/or Grade (GS-or equivalent)</li> </ul> <p><strong>Other Qualifications</strong>:</p> <ul> <li>Job-related training courses (title and year)</li> <li>Job-related skills (e.g., foreign languages, computer software/hardware, etc)</li> <li>Job-related certificates and licenses (current only)</li> <li>Job-related honors, awards, and special accomplishments (e.g., memberships in professional or honor societies, leadership activities, public speaking, performance awards and publications) (give dates)</li> </ul> <p><strong>Privacy Act Requirements</strong>:&nbsp;
