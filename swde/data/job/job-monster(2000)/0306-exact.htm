Principal Database Coordinator

About the Job

Job Title

  • Principal Database Coordinator


Position Description

  • The Principal Database Programmer is responsible for providing technical excellence for data management applications required for clinical studies. The Principal Database Programmer will actively search for solutions which reduce set-up times including increased automation of the set-up and will take a leading role in evaluation or improvement projects regarding data management applications or interfaces between data management systems and other applications. Development and improvement of standards, templates, and process to improve efficiency and quality of databases/database set-up is expected along with a level of technical expertise that evokes innovation.

Position Responsibilities

  • Provides advice to Senior Management in Data Science with respect to software tools, improvements and solutions which would improve efficiency in data review and data cleaning.
  • Defines, develops or improves specifications for database building, system integrations, or validation and devises plans for Global implementation in conjunction with other stakeholders.
  • Provides new and innovative ideas regarding study database design and/or reports and/or process that will improve efficiency, quality, and cost-effectiveness.
  • Maintains and improves Global templates and database setup process through communication with various departments (may include Data Management, Statistics and Programming, Clinical, Medical Writing, Regulatory, and Safety) to ensure databases/templates continue to meet requirements.
  • Coordinates database building, set-up of system integration requirements, or set-up of batch uploaded data.
  • Defines specifications for creation of system interfaces together with stakeholders outside the department.
  • Manages the implementation of new technologies or systems integrations and associated training for internal/external personnel together with line management and relevant stakeholders.
  • Ensures coding dictionaries are functioning as required. Ensures maintenance and improvement of coding applications.
  • Trouble-shoots and solves study building issues, system integration issues (such as IVRS failures, CTMS issues), or change request complications using the appropriate applications.
  • Develops complex standard reports or trouble-shoots and solves report issues in addition to creating study level reports using J Review, Business Objects, and Crystal Reports, etc.
  • Identifies the need for and creates complex custom functions to solve database issues and improve efficiency.
  • Trains department personnel and ensures ongoing training of study builders on new applications which may include system integrations.
  • Trains database building staff at Contract/Clinical Research Organizations (CROs) on company process, core configuration and system specifics.
  • Oversees CROs activities and deliverables.
  • Establishes and maintains processes for vendor oversight. Manages system vendor activities as appropriate.
  • Collaborates with IT (internal and external vendors) on change control and routine system maintenance.
  • Leads Global discussions in Global committees regarding database set-up, core configuration, system integration, eCRF/Global Library, edit checks, derivations, and reports as assigned.
  • Supports database building activities (eCRF screens, change requests, edit checks, special actions/functions, derivations, configures interfaces) as needed.
  • May be required to travel for project meetings and training purposes.
  • Complies with established standard operating procedures (SOPs), EMEA, FDA, and ICH regulations and policies.

Position Requirements

  • lBS/BA degree in computer science. Combination of data management application experience and college courses in a related field may be considered.
  • Experience with pharmaceutical clinical research applications general guidelines: 6+ years.
  • Strong computer skills required, including knowledge of programming, database management software.
  • Advanced knowledge of programming languages (SQL, C#, C++, Java) as well as advanced knowledge of reporting and business intelligence tools like J-Review, Business Objects and Crystal Reports.
  • Solid understanding of relational database structure.
  • Demonstrated experience in leading system selection and implementation projects.
  • Solid understanding of data flow.
  • Good knowledge of systems outside of data management, like safety systems, Clinical Trial Management Systems and also electronic patient reported outcome tools.
  • Ability to learn new technologies quickly and disseminate information.
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills and is capable of working with individuals across multiple disciplines, projects, and regions.
  • Superior organizational skills in order to prioritize work.
  • Ability to manage activities of multiple technical projects and provide training.
  • Knowledge of the drug development process.
  • Knowledge of computer system validation requirements.
  • Demonstrated interpersonal and project management skills.
    Use of Microsoft Office Suite (Word, Excel).
